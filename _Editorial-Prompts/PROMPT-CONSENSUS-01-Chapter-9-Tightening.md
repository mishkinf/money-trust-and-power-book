### PRIORITY 2: Condense Bank Charter Act Mechanics (~300-400 words)

**Current issue**: The explanation of note issuance limits, Banking Department vs. Issue Department, etc. is technically precise but narratively dead.

**What readers NEED to understand:**
- Peel's Act tried to make money = gold by law
- It failed because it ignored bank deposits
- This failure created modern deposit banking (accidentally)

**What readers DON'T need:**
- Detailed mechanics of the two-department system
- Technical discussion of note issuance formulas
- Step-by-step legislative provisions

**Action Required**:
1. Cut the Act explanation from ~800 words to ~400 words
2. Focus on **consequences** not **mechanisms**
3. Use analogy: "Peel tried to lock money in a gold cage, but money simply evolved wings (deposits) and flew out"

**Research Check**: ✓ No new research needed—simplification of existing material
