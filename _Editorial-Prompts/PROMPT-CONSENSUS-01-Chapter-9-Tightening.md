### 2. Colonial Hut Tax Section (Lines 165-175) — CONDENSE 40%
**Current: 11 lines, ~400 words**
**Target: ~240 words**
**Issue**: Over-explained with excessive Kenya specifics

**Keep**:
- The principle (taxation drives currency adoption empirically proven)
- The irony (London economists denied it while colonial administrators proved it)
- One concrete example (Kenya 1901 Hut Tax, 2-3 rupees payable only in colonial currency)

**Cut**:
- Detailed implementation mechanics
- Extended discussion of pre-tax vs. post-tax adoption patterns (readers get it quickly)

**Revised approach**: 
Make this a sharp, punchy example in 6-8 lines. The point is powerful—don't overwork it. The evidence was unambiguous, but metropolitan economic theory ignored what imperial practice demonstrated."
