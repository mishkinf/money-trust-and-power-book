# Editorial Prompt: Add Visual Aids to Technical Sections

## OBSERVATION
**Current Rating: Technical Accessibility 8.5/10**  
**Target Rating: 9.5/10 with strategic visuals**

The manuscript handles complex economics remarkably well through clear prose, concrete examples, and analogies. However, several technical sections would benefit from **visual aids** (charts, diagrams, timelines, tables) that allow readers to grasp relationships instantly rather than parsing dense paragraphs.

**Visual aids are especially effective for:**
- Showing relationships between entities (hierarchies, flows, connections)
- Clarifying sequential processes (money creation steps)
- Comparing multiple cases (monetary systems across eras)
- Illustrating quantitative changes (price movements, debt levels)

**Current strengths:**
- Chapter 5: Balance sheet examples (excellent)
- Chapter 8: Monetary schools comparison table (very good)
- Some chapters have "Understanding Check" text boxes

**Gaps:**
- No timeline graphics showing gold vs. paper money periods
- Missing flow diagrams for complex processes (double-entry, money creation)
- No visual comparison of distributional effects (who wins/loses under different systems)
- Could use maps showing geographic monetary patterns

## GOAL
Add 8-12 strategically placed visual aids that:
1. **Clarify rather than decorate:** Every visual serves pedagogical purpose
2. **Simplify complex concepts:** Make difficult ideas instantly graspable
3. **Complement text:** Visuals work with narrative, not replacing it
4. **Maintain professional tone:** Charts/diagrams match book's serious scholarly approach

**Impact on Sales:** Visual aids significantly improve reader retention in serious nonfiction. Readers who can "see" complex relationships are more likely to finish the book and recommend it.

## HOW TO IMPLEMENT

### Step 1: Identify High-Complexity Sections
Where readers currently must work hardest:
- Technical processes (money creation)
- Quantitative comparisons (prices, debt levels)
- Geographic/temporal scope (gold standard spread)
- Distributional effects (who wins/loses)

### Step 2: Choose Appropriate Visual Type
- **Flowcharts:** Sequential processes
- **Line graphs:** Changes over time
- **Tables:** Comparative analysis
- **Maps:** Geographic patterns
- **Bar charts:** Quantitative comparisons
- **Timelines:** Chronological events

### Step 3: Design for Clarity
- Simple > complex
- Black and white works (many readers prefer print)
- Clear labels, minimal jargon
- Caption explains significance

### Step 4: Integrate with Text
- Reference visuals in narrative: "See Figure 5.1..."
- Place visuals where readers need them
- Ensure text works with OR without visuals (accessibility)
