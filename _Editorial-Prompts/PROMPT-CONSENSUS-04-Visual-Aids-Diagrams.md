### PRIORITY 5: Gold Points Mechanism (Chapter 6)

**Purpose**: Show how shipping costs set limits on exchange rate fluctuation

**Proposed Design**:

```
GOLD POINTS: HOW SHIPPING COSTS LIMITED EXCHANGE RATES

                  $4.90/£ ← Gold Export Point
                           (Ship gold TO Britain; sell for pounds)
                     ↑
                     │  Market exchange rate
                     │  fluctuates in this range
                     │
                     ↓
                  $4.85/£ ← Official Parity
                     │
                     ↓
                  $4.80/£ ← Gold Import Point
                           (Ship gold FROM Britain; buy with pounds)


If dollar weakens beyond $4.90/£:
→ Cheaper to buy gold in US, ship to Britain, sell for pounds
→ Arbitrage brings rate back to $4.85-4.90 range

If dollar strengthens beyond $4.80/£:
→ Cheaper to buy gold in Britain, ship to US, sell for dollars
→ Arbitrage brings rate back to $4.80-4.85 range

The "automatic" mechanism was actually:
- Costly (shipping, insurance, risk)
- Slow (weeks for gold to arrive)
- Relied on private arbitrage, not magic
```

**Placement**: Chapter 6, after explaining gold points in text

**Why Critical**: Demystifies the "automatic" gold standard

**Research Check**: ✓ Shipping costs and gold points already documented in Chapter 6

```
THE TRIFFIN DILEMMA (Bretton Woods System)

World needs dollars for:        BUT confidence in dollar requires:

         ↓                                    ↓

    US MUST                              US MUST NOT

                    ↓
              CONTRADICTION
            System must fail

What happened (1944-1971):
├─ 1944-1958: Dollar shortage (world needs more dollars)
├─ 1959-1965: Dollar glut begins (too many dollars)
├─ 1965-1968: Gold drain accelerates
├─ 1968-1971: Crisis escalates
└─ 1971: System collapses (Nixon closes gold window)

The dilemma was structural—no policy could solve it.
```

## Implementation Specifications

### Design Standards

**Style**: Clean, professional, clear
- Black and white (print-friendly)
- Sans-serif fonts for labels
- High contrast for readability
- Printable at standard book size (6" x 9")

**Placement**:
- Always within 1-2 pages of textual explanation
- Referenced explicitly in text: "(see Figure 5.1)"
- Caption explains what reader should notice

**Accessibility**:
- All information conveyed in caption (don't rely on color)
- Text size readable at printed scale
- Alternative text descriptions for e-book versions

### Production Process

**Step 1: Draft Visuals** (Author/Editor creates rough versions)
- Can use ASCII art initially (as above)
- Or rough sketches with labels
- Key is getting concept and data right

**Step 2: Professional Design** (Publisher's design team)
- Takes rough drafts and makes publication-quality
- Ensures consistency across all diagrams
- Integrates with book's overall design

**Step 3: Author Review**
- Verify accuracy of data
- Confirm visual conveys intended concept
- Check caption clarity
