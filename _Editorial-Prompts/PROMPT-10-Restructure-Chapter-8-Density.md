## HOW TO IMPLEMENT

### Step 1: Identify Analytical vs. Narrative Content
**Analytical (→ text boxes/sidebars):**
- Distributional calculations
- Technical mechanisms
- Comparative tables

**Narrative (→ main text):**
- Attwood's story
- Historical events
- Policy decisions

### Step 2: Create Visual Hierarchy
Use:
- **Bold subheadings** for major sections
- *Italic subheadings* for subsections
- Text boxes for complex analysis
- Tables for comparisons
- White space between major transitions

### Step 3: Test for "Scanning"
Readers should be able to scan the chapter and understand:
- Main argument (from subheadings)
- Key evidence (from visual aids)
- Human stories (from bolded names/dates)

### Step 4: Preserve Depth
Moving material to text boxes doesn't mean cutting it—it means organizing for multiple reading styles:
- **Skimmers:** Get main argument from structure
- **General readers:** Can follow narrative with boxes as reference
- **Deep readers:** Engage fully with analytical details

## WHAT TO PRESERVE

### Intellectual Ambition
Chapter 8's complexity is its strength—readers SHOULD work harder here. The goal is accessibility, not simplification.

### Birmingham School Material
Attwood's story is emotionally powerful and analytically crucial. Don't cut it—structure it better.

### Distributional Analysis
This is the heart of the chapter's argument. Moving it to a text box makes it CLEARER, not less important.

### Scholarly Credibility
The detailed documentation of curriculum capture, media influence, and legislative embedding is what separates this from pop-econ. Maintain the evidence while improving presentation.
