## Style Guidelines (Established in Chapters 1-4)

### Opening Style
- Begin with vivid, concrete scene
- Specific time, place, sensory details
- Introduce chapter's theme through narrative
- Example: Ch 1 (Uruk scribe), Ch 2 (Florentine counting room), Ch 3 (Edward III's empty treasury), Ch 4 (William entering London)

### Narrative Approach
- Explain concepts through historical examples, not abstract theory
- Use concrete numbers and specific dates
- Show rather than tell (e.g., demonstrate MMT principles without naming them)
- Maintain human scale even when discussing large systems

### Section Structure
- Use `---` horizontal rules between major sections
- Section headings in `### format` (h3)
- Each section 3-5 paragraphs
- Build chronologically within chapters

### Citation Style
- Numbered footnotes (superscript numbers)
- Full bibliography at end under "Sources & Further Reading"
- 15-25 sources per chapter
- Mix of primary sources, academic works, and contemporary accounts

### Technical Concepts
- Explain without jargon
- Use balance sheet examples when helpful but keep simple
- Define terms in context
- Assume intelligent but non-specialist reader
