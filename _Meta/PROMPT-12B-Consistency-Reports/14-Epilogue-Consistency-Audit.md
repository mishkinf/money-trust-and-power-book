**Six Essential Lessons:**
1. Credit preceded commodity money (evidence from Mesopotamia, England, China)
2. Taxation drives currency demand (grain credits → tally sticks → fiat)
3. Institutions matter more than metal (Bank of England, Federal Reserve credibility)
4. Domestic and foreign money operate differently (tally sticks vs gold, real vs dollar debt)
5. War drives monetary innovation (Bank of England, Bank Restriction, Fed, Bretton Woods)
6. Flexibility requires discipline (fiat liberation + institutional responsibility)

**Hyperinflation Analysis:**
- Venezuela: Oil production fell 77-90% (2013-2020) BEFORE heavy printing
- Weimar: Mark stabilized at 320:1 mid-1922; hyperinflation only after Ruhr occupation (Jan 1923)
- Zimbabwe: Production collapse (2000) preceded hyperinflation (2007) by 7 years
- Hungary: 50% industrial capacity destroyed, 90% damaged
- Pattern: Productive capacity destruction + foreign currency trap = hyperinflation

**Successful Fiat Evidence:**
- Japan: 250%+ debt-to-GDP, deflation averaging -0.3% (1995-2013)
- US: Fed balance sheet $0.9T → $8.9T, inflation supply-driven not monetary
- Britain 1797-1821: 24 years suspended convertibility, 1.2% average inflation

**CBDC Analysis:**
- 100+ countries exploring
- China digital yuan deployed
- EU digital euro in development
- Promises: financial inclusion (1.7B unbanked), efficiency, precision
- Risks: surveillance, disintermediation, programmability, cyber vulnerability
- Historical parallel: Medieval monarchs with coinage control → debasement

**Argentina-Brazil Comparison:**
- Brazil: Real-denominated debt, floating rates, monetary sovereignty
- Argentina: Dollar-denominated debt, recurring crises (1989, 2001, 2018)
- Lesson: Monetary sovereignty through currency denomination choices

**Future Challenges:**
- Maintaining trust without gold convertibility
- Balancing flexibility and discipline
- International coordination with floating rates
- Technological change (CBDCs) while preserving credibility

**Thematic Synthesis:**
- Money is credit (clay tablets → electronic databases)
- Trust is essential (institutional competence)
- Power matters (creditor/debtor, state/citizen)
- Institutions evolve (temples → merchant houses → central banks)

**Closing Wisdom:**
- Trust institutions, not metal
- Build institutions worthy of trust
- Money is choice, not destiny
- Real resources constrain, not money itself
