### 4. Tax/Spend Framing Made Explicit (IMPORTANT)

Three strategic additions to seed, prove, and apply the inversion:

#### 4a. Preface - Theme #2 (Line 18)

**Added**:
> "**This inverts the common story.** We're told governments must tax or borrow before spending, as if they need to 'find the money.' But historically, sovereigns spent first—issuing tallies, notes, credits—and taxed later to create demand for what they'd issued. The sequence matters: **spend first, tax later** is how money enters circulation. The 'taxes fund spending' story is backwards."

**Why**: Seeds the inversion upfront so readers have the framework before encountering the historical evidence.

#### 4b. Chapter 5 - After Taxation Cycle (Line 52)

**Added**:
> "**This reveals the fundamental inversion in public discourse.** Politicians claim 'we're out of money' and 'must raise taxes to afford programs.' But the government creates money by spending—the Bank's notes entered circulation through government expenditure, not through prior taxation. Taxes don't fund spending; **taxes create demand for what government spends**. The constraint is not finance but real resources: inflation occurs when spending exceeds productive capacity, not when it exceeds tax revenue. The household budget metaphor fails because households must earn before spending—they cannot create money. Governments that issue their own currency can spend before taxing—they create money by spending."

**Why**: Proves the inversion through historical evidence (Bank of England 1694-1797), showing the actual mechanism.

#### 4c. Chapter 8 - Real Constraints Section (Line 202)

**Added**:
> "**This is why the household budget metaphor fails.** Households must earn before spending—they cannot create money. Governments that issue their own currency can spend before taxing—they create money by spending. The constraint for households is finance (income). The constraint for currency-issuing governments is real resources (productive capacity). Confusing these leads to policy disasters: imposing austerity during recessions when resources sit idle, claiming scarcity when the only limit is political will."

**Why**: Applies the lesson to modern context, making policy implications explicit.

**Evaluation verdict**: This was identified as STRONGLY RECOMMENDED to make the book's core argument unmissable. ✅ COMPLETED
