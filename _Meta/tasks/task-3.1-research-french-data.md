## Objective

Gather specific data about French monetary and fiscal history to enable a systematic comparison with England. This will strengthen the manuscript's institutional argument by showing what happened when those institutions were absent.

---

## Research Questions

### 3.1.1: French Default History

**What to find**:
- Specific dates of major French defaults (1648, 1661, and others)
- Amounts involved (in livres or equivalent)
- Which creditors were affected
- Consequences for French credit

**Why it matters**: Shows the pattern of unreliability that made French borrowing expensive.

**Potential sources**:
- Richard Bonney, *The King's Debts: Finance and Politics in France*
- David Bien, "Offices, Corps, and a System of State Credit"
- Philip Hoffman et al., *Priceless Markets*

---

### 3.1.2: Comparative Borrowing Costs

**What to find**:
- Interest rates paid by French crown vs. English crown (1690-1750)
- Specific examples of loans with terms
- How rates changed after 1688 Glorious Revolution in England

**Why it matters**: Quantifies the "credibility premium" that parliamentary control provided.

**Potential sources**:
- David Stasavage, *Public Debt and the Birth of the Democratic State*
- P.G.M. Dickson, *The Financial Revolution in England*
- François Velde & David Weir, "The Financial Market and Government Debt Policy in France, 1746-1793"

---

### 3.1.3: French Tax Farming System

**What to find**:
- How tax farming worked in practice
- Corruption levels and inefficiency
- Comparison with English direct collection
- Revenue collected vs. revenue reaching crown

**Why it matters**: Shows why French fiscal capacity was weaker despite larger population.

**Potential sources**:
- James Collins, *The State in Early Modern France*
- George Matthews, *The Royal General Farms in Eighteenth-Century France*
- J.F. Bosher, *French Finances 1770-1795*

---

### 3.1.4: Banque de France Founding Context

**What to find**:
- Why earlier attempts at French central banking failed (John Law's scheme, etc.)
- What changed after Napoleon that made Banque de France succeed
- Institutional reforms Napoleon imposed
- How it differed from Bank of England model

**Why it matters**: Shows that institutional design mattered more than national characteristics.

**Potential sources**:
- Eugene White, "Making the French Pay: The Costs and Consequences of the Napoleonic Reparations"
- Alain Plessis, *Histoires de la Banque de France*
- Charles Kindleberger, *A Financial History of Western Europe*

---

### 3.1.5: Comparative Fiscal Capacity

**What to find**:
- Tax revenue as percentage of GDP for France vs. England (1690-1750)
- Per capita tax burden
- Efficiency of collection
- What taxes each country could impose

**Why it matters**: Shows that England's advantage was institutional, not based on wealth.

**Potential sources**:
- Patrick O'Brien, "The Political Economy of British Taxation, 1660-1815"
- Philip Hoffman & Kathryn Norberg (eds.), *Fiscal Crises, Liberty, and Representative Government*
- Mark Dincecco, *Political Transformations and Public Finances*
